Butter Chicken

India's favourite — tender chicken in a luscious tomato-butter-cream sauce.

⏱ 45 min 🍽 Serves 4 📊 Medium 🌍 North Indian
← Back to All Recipes

📋 Recipe Info

Prep Time 15 min
Cook Time 30 min
Total Time 45 min
Servings 4 people
Difficulty Medium
Category Non-Veg
Calories ~380 kcal

🛒 Ingredients

  • 500g boneless chicken, cubed
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tsp tandoori masala
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 ripe tomatoes, blended
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 1 tbsp sugar or honey
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander, to garnish

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

Follow these steps carefully for restaurant-quality Butter Chicken at home.

  1. 1

    Marinate the chicken: In a large bowl, mix yogurt, tandoori masala, red chilli powder, and a pinch of salt. Add chicken cubes, coat well, and marinate for at least 30 minutes (overnight is best).

  2. 2

    Grill or pan-sear: Heat 1 tbsp oil in a pan on high heat. Add marinated chicken pieces and sear for 4–5 minutes per side until charred and cooked through. Set aside.

  3. 3

    Make the base: In the same pan, melt butter. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown, about 8–10 minutes.

  4. 4

    Add aromatics: Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook for 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears. Add blended tomatoes and cook on medium heat for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  5. 5

    Spice it up: Add garam masala, red chilli powder (to taste), and sugar. Stir well and cook for 3 more minutes. Season with salt.

  6. 6

    Cream it: Lower the heat. Pour in heavy cream and mix gently. Simmer for 5 minutes — do not boil after adding cream.

  7. 7

    Add chicken & finish: Add the seared chicken pieces to the sauce. Simmer for 5–7 minutes so the chicken absorbs the gravy flavours.

  8. 8

    Serve: Garnish with fresh coriander and a swirl of cream. Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed basmati rice. Enjoy!

💡 Pro Tip

For a silkier sauce, blend the cooked tomato-onion base before adding cream. This makes it restaurant-smooth.
