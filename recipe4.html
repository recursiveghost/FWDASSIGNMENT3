Dal Makhani

Slow-cooked black lentils in a buttery, spiced gravy — the soul of Punjabi home cooking.

⏱ 90 min 🍽 Serves 4 📊 Medium 🌍 Punjabi
← Back to All Recipes

📋 Recipe Info

Prep Time10 min + soak
Cook Time80 min
Total Time~90 min
Servings4 people
DifficultyMedium
CategoryVegetarian
Calories~310 kcal

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole black lentils (urad dal)
  • ¼ cup kidney beans (rajma)
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, puréed
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • ¼ cup heavy cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander, to garnish
  • Extra butter for finishing

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

Good Dal Makhani rewards patience — the longer it simmers, the richer it becomes.

  1. 1

    Soak overnight: Rinse black lentils and kidney beans under cold water. Soak in plenty of water for 8–10 hours or overnight. Drain before cooking.

  2. 2

    Pressure cook: Add soaked lentils and beans to a pressure cooker with 4 cups water, ½ tsp salt, and ½ tsp turmeric. Pressure cook for 5–6 whistles until completely soft. Set aside without draining.

  3. 3

    Make the masala: Heat butter and oil in a heavy-bottomed pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add onions and sauté on medium heat for 12–15 minutes until deep golden.

  4. 4

    Add aromatics: Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook for 2 minutes. Add tomato purée, red chilli powder, and garam masala. Cook for 10 minutes until oil separates from the masala.

  5. 5

    Combine dal & masala: Add the cooked lentils (with their liquid) to the masala. Stir well to combine. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low.

  6. 6

    Slow simmer: Simmer on very low heat for 30–45 minutes, stirring occasionally. The dal should thicken and darken in colour. Add water if it gets too thick.

  7. 7

    Add cream: Stir in heavy cream. Simmer for another 5 minutes. Season with salt.

  8. 8

    Finish & serve: Swirl in a tablespoon of cold butter, garnish with fresh coriander. Serve hot with naan, paratha, or jeera rice.

💡 Pro Tip

For authentic dhaba-style dal, cook in an open pot on the lowest possible flame for 2+ hours, adding a charcoal smoke (dhungar) at the end if available.
