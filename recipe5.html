Pasta Carbonara

Silky egg-and-cheese sauce, crispy pancetta, al dente spaghetti — the classic Roman way.

⏱ 20 min 🍽 Serves 2 📊 Medium 🌍 Italian
← Back to All Recipes

📋 Recipe Info

Prep Time5 min
Cook Time15 min
Total Time20 min
Servings2 people
DifficultyMedium
CategoryNon-Veg
Calories~560 kcal

🛒 Ingredients

  • 200g spaghetti or rigatoni
  • 150g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 egg yolks (extra)
  • 80g Pecorino Romano, finely grated
  • 30g Parmesan, finely grated
  • 2 cloves garlic (optional, not traditional)
  • Freshly cracked black pepper
  • Salt, for pasta water
  • Extra Parmesan, to serve

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

True carbonara has no cream — the silky sauce comes purely from eggs, cheese, and pasta water magic.

  1. 1

    Boil pasta water: Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Salt it generously — it should taste like the sea. Add spaghetti and cook until 1 minute before al dente per the package instructions.

  2. 2

    Make the egg mixture: In a bowl, whisk together 2 whole eggs and 2 extra yolks. Add grated Pecorino Romano and Parmesan. Season with a generous amount of black pepper. Whisk to a thick, pale paste.

  3. 3

    Cook the pancetta: In a large cold pan, add diced pancetta. Cook over medium heat until the fat renders out and the pancetta is golden and crispy. If using garlic, add it here and cook for 1 minute. Remove from heat.

  4. 4

    Reserve pasta water: Before draining pasta, scoop out 1 full cup of starchy pasta cooking water. This is the secret to the silky sauce.

  5. 5

    Combine pasta & pancetta: Drain pasta and add it to the pan with pancetta (off heat or on very low). Toss to coat pasta in the fat. Add a splash of pasta water.

  6. 6

    The critical step — add egg sauce: Remove pan completely from heat. Pour egg-cheese mixture over pasta. Toss rapidly and constantly while adding pasta water tablespoon by tablespoon until a creamy, coating sauce forms. Work quickly — if the pan is too hot, you'll get scrambled eggs, not sauce.

  7. 7

    Serve immediately: Plate at once. Top with extra grated Parmesan and a big crack of freshly ground black pepper. Eat within minutes for the best texture!

💡 Pro Tip

Temperature control is everything. The residual heat from the pasta cooks the eggs just enough to create sauce without scrambling. Never add the egg mixture while the pan is on direct heat.
